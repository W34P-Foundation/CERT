About AMY-CERT

Building resilient communities through trained volunteer response.

Our Mission

AMY-CERT is dedicated to educating and training community members in basic disaster response skills. Our mission is to empower individuals to help their neighbors, families, and communities during emergencies when professional responders may be overwhelmed or delayed.

Through comprehensive training programs, community outreach, and partnership with local emergency services, we build stronger, more resilient neighborhoods that can effectively respond to any crisis.

What We Do

Training Programs

We provide comprehensive CERT training covering fire safety, medical operations, light search and rescue, and team organization. All training is free for community members.

Community Support

Our trained volunteers are ready to assist during disasters, providing immediate support while professional responders address the most critical situations.

Public Outreach

We organize community events, workshops, and presentations to educate residents about emergency preparedness and disaster response.

Coordination

We work closely with local fire departments, emergency management agencies, and other organizations to ensure effective disaster response.

About the CERT Program

The Community Emergency Response Team (CERT) program is a nationally supported initiative that educates volunteers about disaster preparedness and trains them in basic disaster response skills.

Using training learned in the classroom and during exercises, CERT members can assist others in their neighborhood or workplace following an event when professional responders are not immediately available to help.

The CERT program is supported by the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) and is typically sponsored at the local level by fire departments, emergency management offices, or other local government agencies.
